Grafficker Alley is a new festival for self published comics, manga, zines, and artwork, featuring 150 tables showcasing the talented work of creators across the greater Toronto area and beyond!


In partnership with Anime North, Grafficker Alley's aim is to celebrate and promote the grass roots creativity of independent, self-published artists and storytellers. Grafficker Alley will happen September 6 2025, from 11am until 5pm and is free to the public.

About the venue:

Designed in 1940, the Small Arms Inspection Building was used as a large munitions plant for the Canadian Army through the Second World War until 1974. In 2017, the City of Mississauga purchased the building to renovate it into a bright, multi-purpose, open-concept arts and culture venue. Since then, the Small Arms Inspection Building has served as a creative hub for artists in the community and played host to events of all sizes.
